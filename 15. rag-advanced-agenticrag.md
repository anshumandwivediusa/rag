**Agentic RAG** is the evolution of **Retrieval-Augmented Generation** where the retrieval process is no longer static — instead, the LLM acts as an **agent** that can plan, reason, and adapt its retrieval strategy dynamically.  


## 📖 What is Agentic RAG?
- **Agentic Behavior** → The LLM doesn’t just consume retrieved chunks; it actively decides *what to retrieve, how to retrieve, and when to stop*.  
- **Dynamic Querying** → Breaks down complex queries into sub-queries, issues multiple retrieval calls, and merges results.  
- **Tool Use** → Can call external APIs, databases, or graph queries as part of retrieval.  
- **Iterative Refinement** → Uses feedback loops to refine retrieval until confidence thresholds are met.  


## 🗂️ How It Differs from Standard RAG

| Feature | **Standard RAG** | **Agentic RAG** |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| Retrieval | One-shot top‑k similarity | Multi-step, adaptive, agent-driven |
| Query Handling | Static | Recursive decomposition |
| Context | Fixed chunks | Dynamically assembled |
| Reasoning | Limited | Multi-hop, tool-augmented |
| Best Use | FAQs, simple Q&A | Complex reasoning, enterprise AI |


## 🚀 Example
**Query**: *“Summarize the impact of AI regulation in the EU on startups, with examples from 2024–2025.”*

- **Standard RAG** → Retrieves chunks mentioning “AI regulation” + “startups.”  
- **Agentic RAG** →  
  1. Decomposes query: “EU AI regulation,” “impact on startups,” “examples 2024–2025.”  
  2. Calls retriever multiple times for each sub-query.  
  3. Uses external API (e.g., EU law database) for fresh examples.  
  4. Assembles context into a structured answer with citations.  


## 📌 Why Agentic RAG Matters
- Handles **multi-hop reasoning** across large corpora.  
- Reduces hallucinations by enforcing **retrieval-only answers**.  
- Enables **autonomous workflows** (e.g., research assistants, legal copilots).  
- Bridges **RAG + Agentic AI**, making retrieval part of a reasoning loop rather than a static helper.  


## 🛠️ Typical Stack
- **Vector DB**: Milvus, Pinecone, Weaviate  
- **Graph DB**: Neo4j for relationship-aware retrieval  
- **Agent Frameworks**: LangChain Agents, LlamaIndex Agents  
- **LLMs**: Claude, GPT, Grok — orchestrated with agentic control  


## 📌 Takeaway
**Agentic RAG = RAG + Agency.**  
Instead of passively retrieving chunks, the LLM becomes an **active agent** that plans retrieval, queries multiple sources, and assembles nuanced context. This makes it ideal for **enterprise knowledge bases, legal reasoning, and research assistants**.
