# RAPTOR

## 🔑 What is RAPTOR?
RAPTOR is an **advanced retrieval framework** designed to enhance traditional RAG systems. Instead of relying solely on **flat top‑k similarity search**, RAPTOR introduces **recursive, hierarchical, and adaptive retrieval strategies**. This makes it better suited for **complex reasoning tasks** where queries require multi-hop context or layered document understanding.

## 🗂️ Core Concepts
- **Recursive Retrieval** → Breaks queries into sub-queries, retrieves smaller chunks, and recombines them for richer context.  
- **Hierarchical Indexing** → Organizes documents in tree structures (sections → subsections → paragraphs) for multi-level retrieval.  
- **Adaptive Retrieval** → Dynamically adjusts retrieval depth and breadth depending on query type.  
- **Hybrid Search** → Combines semantic vector similarity with symbolic filters (metadata, keywords, graph relations).

| Challenge in Standard RAG | RAPTOR Solution |

| Flat retrieval misses nuance | Recursive decomposition captures subtopics |

| One-shot queries | Multi-step reasoning across documents |

## 🧩 Example Scenario  
**User Query**: *“Explain how the Indian Constitution balances fundamental rights with directive principles.”*

---

### 🔍 Standard RAG Flow
- Embedding query → Top‑k similarity search.  
- Retrieves 3–5 chunks mentioning “fundamental rights” and “directive principles.”  
- LLM answers based only on those chunks.  
- **Problem**: May miss the *relationship* between the two concepts because chunks are flat.

---

### 🦅 RAPTOR RAG Flow
1. **Recursive Decomposition**  
   - Break query into sub-queries:  
     - “What are fundamental rights?”  
     - “What are directive principles?”  
     - “How do they interact?”  

2. **Hierarchical Retrieval**  
   - Pulls context from Constitution sections:  
     - Part III → Fundamental Rights  
     - Part IV → Directive Principles  
     - Case law → Relationship between them  

3. **Adaptive Retrieval**  
   - Since query is comparative, RAPTOR retrieves broader context (legal commentary + case law) instead of just definitions.  

4. **Context Assembly**  
   - Combines retrieved chunks into a structured answer:  
     - Rights = enforceable, individual-focused  
     - Principles = non-enforceable, state policy goals  
     - Courts balance them via “harmonious construction” (e.g., Kesavananda Bharati case).  

---

### 📊 Output Comparison

| Approach | Likely Answer |
|----------|---------------|
| **Standard RAG** | “Fundamental rights are enforceable, directive principles are not.” |
| **RAPTOR RAG** | “Fundamental rights (Part III) are enforceable guarantees, while directive principles (Part IV) guide state policy. Courts balance them through harmonious construction, ensuring neither overrides the other — e.g., Kesavananda Bharati upheld both as essential to constitutional philosophy.” |

---

## 📌 Takeaway
RAPTOR doesn’t just *retrieve chunks* — it **hunts down layered context** (like a raptor bird of prey), decomposes queries, and assembles nuanced answers. This makes it ideal for **multi-hop reasoning, legal research, and enterprise knowledge bases**.
