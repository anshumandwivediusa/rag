## 🎤 Embedding Interview Q&A

**Q1. What is an embedding?**  
**A:** An embedding is a dense vector representation of data that captures semantic meaning and relationships, enabling comparison and retrieval in a continuous vector space.


**Q2. How is embedding different from encoding?**  
**A:** Encoding maps text into token IDs for processing, while embedding maps text into semantic vectors that preserve meaning and similarity.


**Q3. Why are embeddings important in NLP?**  
**A:** They allow models to understand meaning, perform semantic search, enable RAG pipelines, and cluster or classify data based on similarity.


**Q4. How do embeddings capture semantic meaning?**  
**A:** By placing similar concepts close together in vector space — e.g., “king” and “queen” are near, while “cat” and “carburetor” are far apart.


**Q5. How do embeddings handle polysemy (e.g., “bank”)?**  
**A:** Context shifts the embedding — “bank” in finance clusters near money/loan, while “bank” in geography clusters near river/shore.


**Q6. What role do embeddings play in RAG systems?**  
**A:** They convert queries and documents into vectors, enabling similarity search to retrieve the most relevant context for generation.


**Q7. What is cosine similarity and why is it used?**  
**A:** Cosine similarity measures the angle between vectors, making it effective for comparing semantic closeness regardless of magnitude.


**Q8. Dense vs sparse embeddings — what’s the difference?**  
**A:** Dense embeddings capture meaning in high‑dimensional space; sparse embeddings rely on keyword frequency (e.g., TF‑IDF, BM25).


**Q9. What is contextual vs static embedding?**  
**A:** Contextual embeddings change meaning based on sentence context (e.g., “bank” in finance vs river), while static embeddings remain fixed.


**Q10. How do embeddings capture intent beyond keywords?**  
**A:** They emphasize user intent — e.g., “How do I fix a 401 error?” encodes problem‑solving intent, while “What is a 401 error?” encodes definition intent.


**Q11. How do embeddings handle syntax?**  
**A:** They shift based on grammatical role — “The bank approved the loan” vs “The loan was approved by the bank” produce different vectors.


**Q12. What are multilingual embeddings and how are they aligned?**  
**A:** They align vectors across languages so semantically equivalent words (e.g., “dog,” “chien,” “perro”) map close together in the same space.
